Top 5 NBA Players

  1. Kevin Durant
  2. Lebron James
  3. Steph Curry
  4. Kawhi Leonard
  5. James Harden

Kevin Durant

With 2 NBA championships both awarded the MVP, 4x scoring titles as the leagues leading scorer, 2 Olympic gold medals, 9x All-NBA teams, and 11 All-Star appearances, there is no denying the greatness of Kevin Durant. If not for some major injuries many critics believe he would have 4x Championships. The ability for "KD" to be an assest in any scheme or type of teams speaks to his immense basketball skill. There is not one GM in the NBA that wouldn't pay any price to have Durant on their team.

LeBron James

Though coming off an NBA Championship in the 'bubble', LeBron James is much more effective as a coach on the court than the load-bearing wheel of a championship team. When fully engaged there is little argument about who the most dominant player in the NBA is. However, at 37 years of age LeBron isn't able to operate a heightened intensities for a full playoff run. Capable of being a great scorer, facilitator and rebounder, James can fill the statsheet. The problem lies with what doesn't appear, things such as man to man and help defense have become major weakpoints in LeBron's game.

Stephen Curry

The unquestionable greatest shooter in the history of the sport, Stephen Curry has cemented his place in basketball history. Winner of 3x NBA Championships, 2x League MVP's (first ever unanimous), 2x scoring title, 6x All-NBA, and 7x NBA All-Star, there is little question that Curry conquered the 2010's. Interestingly, there were doubts on whether Curry was a beneficiary of the Warriors system or the torch that provided the spark. His ability to get hot at any time in the game, from anywhere on the court, allowed him to propel a talent deficient and injury plagued team to 1 game from the playoffs. Leading the NBA in scoring while being the sole focus of defenses across the league, Steph demonstrated his pure basketball ability.

Kawhi Leonard

Another winner of 2x NBA championships both awarded the MVP, 2x Defensive Player of the Year, 5x All-NBA and All-Star. Kawhi Leonard is arguably the best 'two-way' player in the NBA. His ability to be the teams leading scorer while also defending the opposing teams best offensive weapon speaks to his all world basketball ability. While plagued with injuries since 2017, when healthy Kawhi is a player every coach would love to have.

James Harden

The only member of thistop 5 list to not yet win a championship, James Harden's legacy is still soldified by his penchant for consistently leading the best offenses in the NBA. A league MVP, 3x scoring titles, 7x All-NBA teams, 9x All-Star, and a Assists Leader title provides the critial acclaim for his incomprehensible offensive abilities.
